# 🕯️ Shabbat Calendar 2026 - Swimming Schedule Impact

## Overview

Michael observes Shabbat (Jewish Sabbath) from Friday sunset until approximately one hour after Saturday sunset (Havdalah). During this time, he does not compete in swimming events.

**Key Rule**: No competition from Friday candle lighting through Saturday Havdalah.

### Workarounds
1. **Request time trials**: Some meets offer Thursday/Friday time trials
2. **Swim at different meets**: Choose meets where 50 Free is Friday/Sunday
3. **Focus on 100 Free**: Similar skills, typically Sunday event
4. **High school meets**: FHSAA schedule varies, may have better options

## Communication with Coaches

When discussing Shabbat with college coaches:

> "I observe the Jewish Sabbath, which means I'm unavailable for competition 
> from Friday sunset through approximately one hour after Saturday sunset. 
> In practical terms, this means I can swim:
> - Friday morning sessions (prelims)
> - Friday afternoon sessions (depending on sunset time - better in summer)
> - All Sunday events
> - All weekday events
>
> I understand this may affect Saturday dual meets, but championship meets 
> typically have key events spread across multiple days. I'm happy to discuss 
> how my club and high school have accommodated this successfully."
