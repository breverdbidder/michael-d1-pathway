# 📉 Taper Strategy Guide

## What is Taper?

**Taper** is the strategic reduction in training volume before major competition to allow the body to fully recover and perform at peak capacity.

---

## The Science of Taper

### Physiological Benefits

| System | During Heavy Training | After Taper |
|--------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Muscles** | Micro-tears, fatigue | Rebuilt, stronger |
| **Glycogen** | Depleted | Fully replenished |
| **Nervous System** | Fatigued | Sharp, responsive |
| **Hormones** | Cortisol elevated | Testosterone optimized |
| **Inflammation** | Elevated | Reduced |
| **Sleep Quality** | Often disrupted | Improved |

### Why It Works

1. **Supercompensation**: Body overcompensates for training stress
2. **Neural Recovery**: Reaction time and coordination improve
3. **Muscle Repair**: Complete healing of micro-damage
4. **Energy Restoration**: Full glycogen stores = more power

## Taper Dos and Don'ts

### ✅ DO

- **Maintain intensity**: Keep race-pace work, just less volume
- **Sleep more**: 8-10 hours per night
- **Hydrate**: Extra water and electrolytes
- **Visualize**: Mental rehearsal of races
- **Stretch/Mobility**: Keep muscles loose
- **Eat normally**: Don't dramatically change diet
- **Trust the process**: Feeling "heavy" early in taper is normal

### ❌ DON'T

- **Add volume**: "I feel great!" is a trap
- **Try new things**: Stick to routine
- **Skip practice**: Easy swimming aids recovery
- **Stress**: Mental stress hurts performance
- **Over-analyze**: Trust your training
- **Carb load excessively**: (Especially on keto)
- **Party/Stay up late**: Prioritize rest

## Signs Taper is Working

| Day | Feeling | What It Means |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Days 1-3 | Heavy, sluggish | Normal adaptation |
| Days 4-7 | Antsy, restless | Energy returning |
| Days 8-14 | "Pop" in water | Supercompensation |
| Race Day | Explosive, sharp | PEAK PERFORMANCE |

### Warning Signs

- **Constant fatigue**: May need more rest
- **Getting sick**: Immune system vulnerable - extra sleep, vitamin C
- **Anxiety/Irritability**: Normal, but manage stress
- **Insomnia**: Reduce caffeine, maintain sleep routine

## Race Week Schedule Template

### Thursday (2 days before)
- AM: Easy 2000 yards, focus on feel
- PM: Rest or light dryland

### Friday (1 day before)
- AM: 1500 yards, warm-up routine
- Include: Starts, turns, race-pace 25s
- PM: Rest, visualize, early bed

### Saturday/Sunday (Race Day)
- **Wake**: 3-4 hours before race
- **Eat**: Light breakfast (eggs, small carb if needed)
- **Arrive**: 1.5 hours before warm-up
- **Warm-up**: 800-1200 yards, loosen up
- **Pre-race**: 10-15 min before, get moving
- **RACE**: Execute!
