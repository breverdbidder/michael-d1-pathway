## Why Keto for Swimming?

### Michael Andrew's Protocol

Michael Andrew (Olympic medalist, multiple American records) has been keto-adapted since age 12. Key findings:

1. **Fat Adaptation**: Body efficiently uses fat for fuel during long training sessions
2. **Stable Energy**: No blood sugar spikes/crashes during competition
3. **Body Composition**: Maintains lean muscle mass with lower body fat
4. **Recovery**: Reduced inflammation, faster recovery between events
5. **Mental Clarity**: Consistent focus without carb-induced brain fog

### Scientific Basis

- **Aerobic base training**: Fat metabolism supports long aerobic sets
- **Sprint events**: Glycogen stores (even on keto) sufficient for 50-200 events
- **Ketone production**: Ketones provide efficient brain fuel for focus

## Kosher Considerations

### Protein Sources (Meat Meals)
- ✅ Beef (kosher slaughtered)
- ✅ Chicken/Turkey (kosher slaughtered)
- ✅ Lamb (kosher slaughtered)
- ❌ Pork (not kosher)
- ❌ Shellfish (not kosher)

### Protein Sources (Dairy Meals)
- ✅ Fish (with fins and scales)
- ✅ Eggs
- ✅ Cheese (kosher certified)
- ✅ Greek yogurt (full fat, unsweetened)

### Separation Rules
- Wait 6 hours between meat and dairy
- Fish can be eaten with dairy
- Eggs are pareve (neutral)

## Race Day Protocol

### Morning Meet (Prelims)

**Night Before**
- Normal keto dinner
- Extra hydration
- Early bedtime (9 PM)

**Race Morning (2-3 hours before)**
- 2-3 eggs
- 1/2 avocado
- Small handful of macadamia nuts
- Black coffee (if tolerated)

**Between Events**
- Water with electrolytes
- Small protein snack if >2 hours between races
- Avoid heavy eating

### Afternoon/Evening Finals

**Day of Finals**
- Normal keto breakfast
- Light keto lunch (4+ hours before racing)
- Pre-race: nuts or cheese 2 hours before
- Warm-up: water only

### Exception: Big Championship Meets

For major meets (Sectionals, Futures, Senior Champs), consider **targeted carbs**:
- Small amount of easily digestible carbs (banana, rice cake) 30-60 min before race
- This is optional and should be tested in practice first
- Michael Andrew does NOT do this, but some keto athletes benefit

## Hydration Protocol

### Daily Targets

| Timing | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Morning | 16-24 oz | Upon waking, before food |
| Pre-Practice | 16 oz | 30 min before |
| During Practice | 20-32 oz | Every 15-20 min |
| Post-Practice | 24 oz | Within 30 min |
| Throughout Day | 40-60 oz | Sip regularly |
| **Total Daily** | **100-150 oz** | Adjust for heat/intensity |

### Electrolytes

Keto increases electrolyte needs:
- **Sodium**: 3,000-5,000 mg/day (salt food liberally)
- **Potassium**: 3,500-4,700 mg/day (avocado, leafy greens)
- **Magnesium**: 300-400 mg/day (supplement if needed)

**Recommended**: LMNT, Keto Chow electrolytes, or homemade (salt + lite salt + water)

## Tracking Metrics

### Weekly Weigh-In
- Same time, same conditions (morning, fasted)
- Target: Stable weight or slight gain (muscle)
- Alert if: >2 lb loss in one week

### Monthly Body Composition
- Estimate body fat % (visual or calipers)
- Goal: Maintain lean mass, appropriate body fat for age

### Energy/Performance Log
- Rate daily energy 1-10
- Note any "bonking" during practice
- Track race times for correlation

## Red Flags to Watch

| Symptom | Possible Cause | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Constant fatigue | Undereating, not enough fat | Increase calories/fat |
| Muscle cramps | Electrolyte deficiency | Add sodium/potassium/magnesium |
| Declining times | Energy deficit | Add calories or targeted carbs |
| Brain fog | Dehydration or low sodium | More water + salt |
| Poor sleep | Low magnesium or overstimulation | Magnesium, reduce caffeine |
